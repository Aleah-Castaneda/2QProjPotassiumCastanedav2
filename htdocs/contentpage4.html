Blood Compact Shrine

The Blood Compact Shrine was created to commemorate a friendship alliance in Philippine history between Miguel Lopez de Legazpi of Spain and Datu Sikatuna of Bohol. Visitors can have a good view of the sea and contour of that side of the island of Bohol by standing on the dais. It is located along a national highway with numerous shops offering souvenirs and gifts both along and across the highway.

Activities

Panoramic sceneries

Foreigners and local tourists visit the site for a few minutes to take photos and admiring the panoramic view of the neighborhood. The Blood Compact Shrine of Bohol, which is surrounded by beautiful greeneries of shrubs and trees, can get congested at times, especially when a large group of students is present. If you plan on visiting the region, get there early in the morning to avoid the crowds.

Souvenir shops

Tourists may buy souvenirs at a number of nearby souvenir stores located along and across the highway. Bohol crafts & decors, Tarsier dolls, shells, pearls, jewelry, handbags, and other products are available for purchase.

Historical Knowledge

Students, locals, and international tourists may learn about a significant period in Philippine history when foreign friendship relations were formed. Napoleon Abueva, a national artist, sculpted five life-size bronze statues portraying men gathering around a table toasting beverages with Legazpi and Sikatuna. This gesture represents peace and friendship between foreigners and Bohol residents.

Map and Location

When is the best time to visit?

  • Because the monument is outside, the ideal time to visit is between December and March, when the weather is often cooler. During these months, there are lower chances of rain, allowing you to get a closer look at the memorial.
  • Travelers from Tagbilaran City can reach the historical landmark by driving along Tagbilaran East Road.
  • You may ride a tricycle directly to the monument if you use public transit.
  • You may also join tours that include the Blood Compact Monument as part of their itinerary.
