Mayon Volcano

Mayon Volcano or Mount Mayon is the most iconic volcano in the Philippines due of its perfect cone shape. Its majestic view provides a breathtaking backdrop wherever you are in Albay. Cagsawa Ruins, Mayon 4x4 ATV Ride, and Mayon Volcano Hike/Trek are some tourist spots and exciting activities worth visiting and trying out.

Activities

Hiking Mayon Volcano

Seeing Mayon Volcano in person is amazing, but seeing it up close is much more so! If you’re up for an adventure, you can hike and trek the Mayon Volcano to see it closely. You may choose between two trails: the Buyuhan trail and the Buang trail. Also, keep in mind that the walk might take up to two days. However, you should first contact the Department of Tourism in Legazpi City before hiking Mayon Volcano.

Camping around Mayon Volcano

Hikers commonly camp in the area since climbing Mayon Volcano can take up to two days. You may set up your tent and enjoy a camp meal together with your buddies.

ATV Riding

The adrenaline-pumping ATV ride in Legazpi is the most thrilling way to discover the foothills, ruins, rice fields, rivers, and mud roads strewn with hundreds of volcanic pebbles. Drive an ATV to the Mayon Volcano's Lava Wall. You'll go across valleys, rivers, and falling rock on this ultimate adventure rush.

Safety

  • Strict safety precautions should be observed especially when going on a hike/trek up Mayon Volcano in the Philippines. It is very important to hire a guide when exploring the trails and hiking since they are knowledgeable about weather conditions and things to do in case an unexpected volcanic activity takes place.

    • What to Wear

  • Whether you're riding 4x4 ATV or hiking, be sure to bring all necessary gear and hiking essentials. Make the most of your trip to Mayon Volcano by checking out other tourist spots in Legazpi and neighboring cities or towns.

    • By Flight

  • Traveling from Manila's Ninoy Aquino International Airport (NAIA) to Legazpi Airport takes around 45 minutes on airlines such as Philippine Airlines and Cebu Pacific.

    • By Bus

  • The journey from Manila to Legazpi is about a nine-hour bus ride. Make sure to book in advance via the go-to bus lines. The journey lasts for 9 hours.

    • By Ferry

  • Visitors coming from Leyte or Cebu can take the 12-15 hour journey to Legazpi, Albay via WG&A and Trans Asia shipping lines, both of which sail from Masbate Port.
