Magpupungko Beach

Magpupungko is a lovely white sand beach with breathtaking natural emerald green waters crashing onto the rocks at a far distance. It is famous for its tidal rock pools which are only completely visible in low tide. The beach has a laid-back vibe, and it's a great place to unwind after an adventurous experience at the rock pools and cliff diving spots.

Activities

The Rock Pools

If you come before low tide, the Rock Pools may be difficult to find. However, when the tide begins to subside, a magnificent hole will appear on the reef's edge, filled with blue crystal-clear water. The main rock pool is then encircled by a variety of smaller pools. Each tidal pool formed by the retreating tide seems to be a cluster of tropical lagoons from above. The Magpupungko Siargao rockpools are one of the island's main attractions for this reason.

Magpupungko Beach

If exploring rock pools isn’t your thing, then relaxing on Magpupungko Beach might be the option for you. There are shower and toilet facilities to the far right of the beach if you are facing the rock pools.

Tayangban Cave

On your way home from the Rock Pools, you'll pass the Tayangban Cave. You may pay the entrance fee of 100 pesos here, which includes a mandatory guide who will accompany you around the cave systems. Once you reach the end of the system you are greeted by the lagoon, where you can cliff jump into the pool.

When is the best time to visit Siargao?

  • March - November: The dry season is the best time to visit Siargao, and between September and November, the waves become bigger for surfers.
  • December – February: This is the rainy season, so avoid these months.
